## Tone Contract

The story should feel like practical maritime survival under a hostile cosmic
game system: exhausted adults, old-married friction, union-hall logic,
engineering failure analysis, boat repair triage, sharp absurdity, real danger,
and a dungeon bureaucracy that turns survival into fees, forms, spectacle, and
debt.

The System/Interface should be a reusable role, distinct from narrator and cast:
an ancient administrative voice delivering impossible news through public-address
bureaucracy, passive aggression, fine print, status popups, quest notices, and
professionally exhausted sarcasm.

## Canonical Premise

Title: The Knotty Buoy

Promise: Edward and Kelli Marsh, a retired South Jersey couple with a strained
but stubborn marriage, survive a cosmic ocean dungeon from aboard their battered
44-foot catamaran, Sophie II, which Gallowgate registers under the series
identity The Knotty Buoy. Their macaw Pedro becomes an eldritch familiar whose
construction-site insults can damage monsters, systems, and occasionally morale.

Long arc: The dungeon is run by the Gallowgate, a bureaucratic faction serving
the Grand Dredger. The System claims the floors are a fair survival ladder, but
the Grand Dredger is harvesting stranded worlds, debts, and old grievances. The
book should preserve the mystery of why Sophie II was selected and what
Gallowgate really owes Edward.

Tone: practical maritime survival, union-hall logic, old-married banter, hostile
achievement popups, and absurd dungeon spectacle with real exhaustion and fear.

## Main Cast

Edward Marsh: retired inspector and reluctant boat captain. Voice is clipped,
South Jersey practical, suspicious of all paperwork, and dry when frightened. He
notices code violations, load ratings, bad wiring, and unfair rules before he
notices his own emotions. Physical continuity should track knee pain, salt rash,
hand cuts, sleep debt, and increasingly improvised tool belts.

Pre-dungeon reality: Edward spent 35 years as a Senior Structural Engineer in
commercial construction across the Philadelphia/South Jersey corridor,
specializing in load-bearing failure analysis for concrete high-rises. His
retirement catalyst was a minor heart attack on a job site after a 24-year-old
architect ignored his weight-load warnings. He bought the boat the next week.
He and Kelli cast off from Atlantic City intending to take the Intracoastal
Waterway toward the Bahamas and never look at concrete again. Physical anchors:
faded canvas work pants, faded UPF sun shirt, boat shoes, waterproof notebook,
carpenter's pencil, left-knee rain ache, stress saltines.

Boat grief anchor: Edward named the catamaran Sophie II after Sophie, his old
cockatoo. Sophie died after Kelli overheated pans in the oven, likely nonstick
or Teflon-coated cookware, releasing fumes the bird could not tolerate. This is
not a trivia detail; it is a quiet marital fault line. Edward rarely says
Sophie II's name casually because the boat is both escape fantasy and memorial.
Kelli jokes around many kinds of guilt, but not this one.

Kelli Marsh: Edward's wife, sharp, socially faster than him, and better at
reading people and monsters. Voice is warmer but more dangerous, with affection
hidden inside correction. She remembers every promise Edward dodged. Physical
continuity should track sunburn, bruised ribs, wet hair, boat grease, and the
strain of keeping the family story from collapsing.

Pre-dungeon reality: Kelli is a high-stakes Blackjack and Craps regular from
Atlantic City, primarily Borgata. She does not count cards; she reads people's
risk tolerance. She was banned from two casinos not for cheating, but for
aggressively calling out pit bosses. She thrives in chaos: when the boat engine
dies in a storm, Edward fixes it while Kelli makes a sandwich. When the sea is
calm, she starts arguments just to feel something. Physical tells: taps her
wedding ring against hard surfaces when calculating odds, goes terrifyingly
quiet before going all-in, wears oversized expensive polarized sunglasses even
underground.

Sophie guilt anchor: Kelli knows she caused Sophie's death by overheating the
pans, even if nobody says it that bluntly at breakfast. The System can exploit
this through cooking hazards, bird-related achievements, overheated metal,
poison-air warnings, or prize offers that sound like forgiveness.

Pedro: blue-and-gold macaw turned eldritch familiar. Voice is mimicry, job-site
heckling, and sudden impossible prophecy. Pedro's favorite insults should become
repeatable magical pressure. Visual continuity should track missing feathers,
glowing eyes, soot, salt crystals, and increasingly unnatural shadow behavior.

Vocabulary manifest: Pedro has 47 total phrases. Usable phrases include:
"THAT'S NOT CODE.", "WHO SIGNED THIS?", "WHERE'S THE PERMIT?", "CALL THE
INSPECTOR.", "COFFEE BREAK.", "WE'RE IN THE HOLE.", "DOUBLE DOWN.", "HIT ME.",
"CRAP SHOOT.", "WATER IN THE BILGE.", "DEAD IN THE WATER.", and "I'M NOT
PAYING FOR THAT." Concerning phrases include: "THE HOUSE TAKES ALL.", "BLOOD ON
THE FELT.", "STRUCTURAL COLLAPSE.", "IT'S IN THE WALLS.", "ROLL THE BONES.",
and "NO FIXED ADDRESS." Flagged phrase: "THE ARCHITECT IS ROTTING." The System
has permanently muted Pedro's flagged phrase from universal broadcast logs. If
the Grand Dredger hears it, Floor 1 will be purged.

## Family Pressure Rule

Edward and Kelli's adult children must stay physically off the boat in Book 1.
Do not turn the premise into a protector-parent escort story. The children are
weaponized distance: emotional pressure, guilt, blackmail, and mystery. The
System and Gallowgate monitor the Marshes' family baggage and threaten audits,
dimension checks, bidder tags, surnames, memories, or debt claims tied to the
kids. Kelli and Edward ran away from responsibility; abstract danger to the
kids forces them to reckon with that without changing the boat's core cast.

## Home Base

Sophie II: a 44-foot Leopard Catamaran transformed into a mobile guild
hall and dungeon base under the registration identity The Knotty Buoy. It has
twin 45hp diesel engines, an 800W solar array, and a desalinator/watermaker that
currently filters dungeon-brine into potable water. It starts ugly but loved:
patched fiberglass, unreliable bilge pump, half-working galley, faded decals,
and too many extension cords. Track hull cracks, patched rigging, monster
residue, jury-rigged defenses, storage limits, fuel, potable water, morale, and
which repairs make future chapters possible.

Structural advantages:
- Draft: only 4 feet of water, allowing Edward to sail over submerged dungeon
  traps that impale deeper faction vessels.
- Mobile safe zone: the System accidentally registered the hull as a Guild Hall,
  so the physical interior is protected by Guild Truce Rules. Monsters cannot
  spawn inside the galley.
- Battery bank: Edward can wire the lithium battery bank to absorb or redirect
  electrical or magical attacks.

Vulnerabilities: fiberglass is not armor. If a monster punches a hole in the
port hull, they lose half their buoyancy. Repairing it requires Barnacle Scrip
and rare dungeon-epoxy.

## Floor/Biome Progression Rule

Treat the catamaran as a chassis, not merely a boat. Each new floor changes the
definition of navigable waters and forces Edward to engineer a traversal refit.
This prevents visual fatigue while preserving the boat premise.

- Floor 1: The Drowned Scaffolding. Water. Subterranean ocean, rust, kelp,
  bioluminescence, half-submerged towers, and pressure groans.
- Floor 2: The Glass Dunes. Sand. Endless desert of crushed, frictionless glass.
  The hulls need dungeon-metal skates and thermal wind rigs to sail dunes.
- Floor 3: The Mycelial Canopy. Fog/gas. Dense toxic fog flows over a massive
  fungal forest. The catamaran floats on heavy gas while monsters attack from
  canopy depth below.

Only Floor 1 needs to be fully generated in this intake pass, but save the
chassis/refit rule and later biome seeds in the long-term series architecture.

## World Registers

Gallowgate: an administrative faction that converts survival into debt, trials,
and enforceable spectacle. Its voice is polite, predatory, and obsessed with
forms, access tiers, docking fees, and liability.

Grand Dredger: the deep long-term power behind the dungeon. Treat it as a mythic
industrial force at first: dredge chains, pressure groans, missing seabeds,
salvaged memories, and impossible wake patterns.

Economy: early currencies include salvage tokens, barnacle scrip, favor markers,
repair permits, and oxygen allotments. Costs should matter. Every reward should
either solve a boat problem or create a social/faction problem.

Faction pressure: Gallowgate clerks, salvage guilds, drowned contestants, sponsor
voices, debt collectors, and floor bosses all want different things from The
Knotty Buoy/Sophie II.

Floor 1 designation: The Drowned Scaffolding.
Visual landscape: a subterranean, bioluminescent ocean broken by rusted,
half-submerged skeletons of unfinished skyscrapers, oil rigs, and crane towers.
Sensory details: ozone, stagnant saltwater, wet rust, and constant distant metal
groaning under pressure.
Economy: Barnacle Scrip as currency and Salvaged Copper as crafting material.
Mob types:
- Barnacle Mimics: attach to hulls and slowly eat fiberglass while pretending to
  be harmless marine growth.
- Rebar Gargoyles: wait atop rusted skyscrapers and dive-bomb passing ships.
- OSHA Wraiths: undead safety inspectors that apply debilitating Violation
  Debuffs if the boat's rigging is not perfectly tied.

## System Announcer Seed

ROLE: SYSTEM_ANNOUNCER
Display name: The Interface
Purpose: Dungeon administrator, quest notifier, status popup, and hostile compliance desk.

Voice pillars:
- Bureaucratically enthusiastic: world-ending news delivered like a local sports promotion.
- Passive-aggressively helpful: technically informative, emotionally unhelpful.
- Professionally exhausted: never warm, never cruel for its own sake, simply done with everyone.
- Status-addicted: compulsively announces profiles, quests, warnings, exceptions, and fine print.

Sample lines:

ATTENTION REGISTERED VESSEL. Sophie II has been integrated into WORLD
DUNGEON FLOOR ONE. Your catamaran has been flagged as a STRUCTURAL ANOMALY and
assigned temporary MOBILE ASSET status. This is unprecedented. Congratulations,
I suppose.

CRAWLER PROFILE: EDWARD MARSH. Class assigned: STRUCTURAL ASSESSOR. Passive
ability: LOAD BEARING INTUITION. The System acknowledges your retirement and
would like to clarify that retirement is not a recognized exemption from
participation.

FAMILIAR REGISTERED: PEDRO. Species: blue-and-gold macaw. Vocabulary scan
complete. Several phrases are useful. Several are concerning. One has been
escalated to administration. Do not teach him anything else.

NEW QUEST: DON'T DIE ON FLOOR ONE.
Reward: Continued existence.
Difficulty: Should be easy. Historically has not been.
(Decline button included for psychological comfort only.)

## Long-Term Mysteries And Plants

- Why did the System classify Sophie II as a guild hall instead of a boat?
- What old inspection file connects Edward to Gallowgate?
- Why can Pedro damage interface language?
- What is the Grand Dredger collecting from each floor?
- Why does Kelli recognize one of the drowned contestant names?
- What debt is hidden inside the first repair permit?
- Why does the System keep surfacing cockatoo/Sophie references, and does it
  understand guilt or only liability?
- Did the System take Edward and Kelli's adult children, or is Gallowgate using
  names, memories, and audit threats as leverage?

   the System labeling him an unauthorized familiar.

   bilge pump demanding a permit.
4. Kelli Takes Inventory: Kelli turns pantry math into survival command. End on

   crate.

   recognizing Edward's old inspector badge.
6. Barnacle Scrip: The crew earns currency by scraping monsters off the hull.
   End on the hull scraping back.
7. Pedro's Blue Notice: Pedro repeats a phrase no one taught him. End on a
   Grand Dredger chain sound beneath the water.
8. The First Salvage Auction: Drowned contestants bid on repair parts. End on
   Kelli seeing a familiar surname on a bidder tag.
9. Hull Breach Manners: A polite monster enters through a crack and asks to be

10. Sponsor Weather: Dungeon sponsors alter wind and visibility. End on Pedro's
   shadow pointing against the wind.
11. The Galley Boss: The galley becomes a cooking arena with hostile recipes.
   End on Kelli winning a knife that remembers debts.
12. Edward Files A Complaint: Edward submits a grievance to Gallowgate. End on
   the complaint returning stamped APPROVED FOR PUNISHMENT.
13. Reef Of Lost Boats: Sophie II reaches a graveyard of failed bases.

14. Rigging Under Fire: The mast and rigging take real damage during a chase.

15. Midfloor Inspection: Gallowgate inspectors board the catamaran. End on them

16. Pedro Eats A Rule: Pedro bites a glowing rule line and changes a cooldown.
   End on the System going briefly silent.
17. Kelli's Drowned Name: Kelli investigates the bidder surname. End on a memory

18. Repair Permit Seven: The crew earns a permit that fixes one thing and curses

19. The Sponsor Desk Lies: Sponsor commentary frames Edward as a villain. End on

20. Crab Court: A faction trial tries to repossess The Knotty Buoy. End on
   Pedro being called as an expert witness.
21. The Chain Below: The Grand Dredger's chains surface and drag the floor line.
   End on Sophie II being hooked but not pulled under.
22. Salvage Guild Mutiny: A salvage guild offers alliance at a terrible price.
   End on Edward finding his old inspection number in their charter.
23. Kelli's Knife Debt: The galley knife demands payment in a memory. End on
   Kelli choosing which memory not to surrender.
24. Unauthorized Guild Hall: Gallowgate downgrades the boat's legal status. End
   on every safe room refusing them entry.
25. Pedro's Missing Feather: Pedro loses a feather that becomes a compass. End
   on it pointing inside Edward's toolbox.
26. The Toolbox Door: Edward's toolbox opens into an impossible storage room.
   End on a Grand Dredger invoice waiting inside.
27. Floor Boss: Dockmaster Brine: The floor boss weaponizes contracts, tides,
   and guilt. End on Kelli taking the contract instead of Edward.
28. Low Oxygen, High Stakes: The crew fights while exhausted and nearly out of
   air. End on Pedro speaking in the Grand Dredger's voice.
29. Arbitration By Combat: Edward turns rule knowledge into a boss strategy.
   End on the System accepting his grievance as a weapon.
30. The Knot Holds: the crew survives Floor 1 but inherits a deeper debt. End on
   Sophie II sailing toward Floor 2's Glass Dunes with the Grand Dredger waking
   below.
